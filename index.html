Decoding decisions with a gated-recurrent VAE

A variational autoencoder that reads a monkey's chosen object, along four visual feature dimensions, from 300+ simultaneously recorded intracranial channels. About 62% balanced accuracy across all four features (roughly 15% above chance once chance-corrected), and about 80% when decoding is restricted to the rewarded feature.

The interpretability bridge

Feature attribution on my own decoders

Shuffle a neural input, a whole brain region, or a latent unit, and measure how far decoding accuracy falls. Validated with Monte Carlo and permutation testing, this located area-specific contributions to the decode. It is the same question ablation and activation patching ask of artificial networks.
